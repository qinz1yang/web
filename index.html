About Me

I am a senior mathematics student at Cornell University with a strong interest in geometric analysis, partial differential equations, and their applications. My research experience spans differential geometry, Bayesian networks, and mathematical modeling.

Research Interests

  • Differential Geometry
  • Partial Differential Equations
  • Geometric Analysis
  • Bayesian Networks and Probabilistic Modeling

Current Focus

I'm currently exploring Morse Theory under the supervision of Professor Xin Zhou and working as a book assistant for "Differential Geometry and Applications" by Richard Hamilton, Monique Chyba, and Xiaodong Cao.

My recent work includes proofs of the Fary-Milnor Theorem and the Isoperimetric Inequality, along with developing a Python toolbox for Dynamic Bayesian Networks at the Cornell Design and Augmented Intelligence Lab.
